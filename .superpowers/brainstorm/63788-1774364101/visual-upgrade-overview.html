Visual Upgrade: Approach A Overview

Six changes that transform the scene from "Minecraft" to professional configurator

1

HDRI Environment Map

Replace flat ambient light with a real sky environment. Adds natural light bounce from all directions, realistic reflections on glass and metal surfaces. Using @react-three/drei's Environment component with a free outdoor HDRI from Poly Haven.

2

Post-Processing Pipeline

Add @react-three/postprocessing with three effects:
SSAO — soft shadows in corners and where surfaces meet (kills the flat look)
Tone Mapping — ACESFilmic for cinematic color response
Bloom — subtle glow on glass and bright surfaces

3

Remove Hard Edge Lines

Delete all <Edges> components from walls. These black wireframe lines are the single biggest "game engine" tell. Selection feedback will use emissive glow only (already works).

4

Ground Plane Overhaul

Replace the 25×25 floating box with a much larger ground plane (100×100) that fades softly into the sky at the edges. Add soft contact shadows beneath buildings using Drei's ContactShadows.

5

Renderer & Shadow Tuning

Enable ACESFilmic tone mapping on the WebGL renderer, bump shadow map to 4096×4096, add shadow bias tuning to eliminate shadow acne. Soft shadow edges via SoftShadows from Drei.

6

Material Refinement

Tune envMapIntensity per material type so wood stays matte while glass and metal pick up environment reflections. Adjust roughness/metalness values to better respond to the new HDRI lighting.

New Dependencies

  • @react-three/postprocessing — post-processing effects (SSAO, bloom, tone mapping)
  • postprocessing — peer dependency for the above

No other new deps needed — Environment, ContactShadows, SoftShadows are all already in @react-three/drei.

💡 Mobile strategy: Detect GPU tier at startup. Desktop gets full SSAO + bloom + 4096 shadows. Mobile gets contact shadows only + 2048 shadows, skipping the expensive post-processing pass.
